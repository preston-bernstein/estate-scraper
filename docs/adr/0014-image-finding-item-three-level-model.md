# 0014 — Image / Finding / Item: three-level data model

A single listing photo can contain several distinct objects (a Stickley chair *and* a sterling tray), and the durable training corpus needs the photos the vision pass *skipped* as much as the ones it flagged. A two-table `findings(description) → sales` shape — where the embedding and the free-text identification both hang off a flagged image — cannot express either fact, so the model is split three ways. **Image** is every analyzed photo and owns the irreplaceable image-level facts (embedding, thumbnail, phash, position); it is the substrate the taste ranker trains on, and the junk images that never become Findings live here as the negative exemplars [ADR 0013](0013-persist-embeddings-thumbnails-at-scan-time.md) requires. **Finding** is the flagged subset — "the model thought this photo had value" — and references an Image. **Item** is one identified object inside a Finding, carrying the normalized, queryable identification (`maker`, `category`, `era`, `desirability`) that [ADR 0011](0011-value-aware-identifier-comps-deferred.md) wants for browse-history and future comps joins.

The consequence that justifies the extra table: embeddings and thumbnails move onto **Image, not Finding** — they are image-level facts true of every photo, and putting them on Finding would leave the bulk-negative training half (junk images, confirmed by "waste" Outcomes per [ADR 0015](0015-asymmetric-outcome-propagation.md)) with nowhere to attach. Normalized identification moves onto **Item, not Finding** — because one image holds many objects, `maker`/`category` cannot be columns on the image-level row without collapsing multi-item photos to a single guess. The alternative of keeping identification as a free-text `findings.description` blob was rejected: it makes the browse facet and the comps join impossible (you cannot `WHERE maker = 'Stickley'` against a sentence). The free-text `description` is retained on Finding as a human-readable summary and provenance trail, with the structured fields living one level down on Item.
