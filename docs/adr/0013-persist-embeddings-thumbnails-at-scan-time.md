# 0013 — Persist embeddings and thumbnails at scan time; source listings expire

Estate sale listings are removed from estatesales.net days after the sale ends, and the `picturescdn` image URLs then 404. The flywheel's most valuable training pair — `(image, Outcome)` — is assembled across that expiry: the image is seen at scan time (Friday), but the user's Outcome is authored weeks later, after attending. Storing only `findings.imageUrl` (a CDN pointer) means the image half of every pair has evaporated by the time the verdict half exists. The decision is therefore to persist, at scan time, the **CLIP/SigLIP embedding for every analyzed image** (not just findings — junk-image embeddings are the negative exemplars that teach the Tier-1 ranker what to skip) and a downscaled **thumbnail**, so both halves of the pair survive the listing going down.

The cost is small and the embedding is already computed for Tier-1 ranking, so storing it is reuse, not extra work: ~6k embeddings/scan ≈ 18 MB (~1 GB/year) as a `BLOB` in the canonical SQLite, and ~6k thumbnails/scan ≈ 90 MB (~4.7 GB/year) as plain files on the NAS, referenced by path. This makes embeddings and thumbnails **irreplaceable** (tied to expired sales) rather than regenerable, placing them in the durable tier that gets backed up. The alternative — treating each scan as throwaway and re-fetching images on demand — was rejected because it is impossible: the source data is gone, so no cross-week training corpus could ever be built and the learn-your-taste premise of [ADR 0011](0011-value-aware-identifier-comps-deferred.md) would never have data to stand on.
